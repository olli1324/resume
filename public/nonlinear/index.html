▎ Why this plan is built around recipes and recognition

Six methods, each with its own three-line recipe

The exam is multiple choice plus a few open questions, written in MATLAB/Simulink. The failure mode is method confusion under time pressure: Jacobian vs feedback linearization, Lyapunov vs LaSalle, sliding mode vs NMPC, linear KF vs EKF, DCM vs quaternion, kinematics vs dynamics.

So each topic page leads with the one-line tell that separates it from its neighbours, then walks through the recipe you need to reproduce on paper or in Simulink. The quiz drills classification and small derivations; the flashcards hammer the confused pairs. The aerospace block is half the question pool by syllabus weight, so do not skimp on LS01–LS06.

Exam-day tactics

Computer lab-based, 2 to 2:15 hours, 7 to 11 questions. Multiple choice plus a few open questions with MATLAB/Simulink. Wrong answers carry a small negative score, so do not guess blind.

  • Read all questions first. Mark each as E (easy), M (medium), or H (hard). Clear all the E's first.
  • About 12 to 18 minutes per question average. Anything over 20 minutes, flag and move on.
  • Allowed material: teacher-selected slides, MATLAB libraries used in the course. Download these before you start.
  • The lab work counts toward the final grade, so the labs you did this semester already give you a baseline.
  • For the open questions, build the Simulink first, then check it before writing the explanation.
