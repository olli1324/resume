▎ Why this plan is built around recognition

The failure mode is concept confusion, not missing knowledge

You have seen all 15 lectures already. Re-watching them is the slow path. The two previous attempts did not fall short on time or on guessing penalties — they fell short because similar concepts blurred together under pressure: LP vs QP vs SOCP, convex set vs convex function, primal vs dual, gradient vs Newton, KKT vs Lagrangian.

So the work here is disambiguation, not re-reading. Each topic page leads with the one-line tell that separates it from its neighbours. The quiz drills classification under time. The flashcards hammer the confused pairs. Re-derivation is reserved for the few things that actually need it — KKT, the dual of a small QP, an SVD by hand.

The five-week schedule

Roughly 5–10 hours a week. Diagnose first, then drill recognition, then time yourself on past papers.

Weeks 1–3 — build and disambiguate

  • Week 1: diagnostic under timed conditions, then rebuild the linear-algebra bedrock (gradients, Hessians, SVD, PSD).
  • Week 2: convexity recognition and standard forms. Classify any problem as LP / QP / SOCP / GP / SDP within 30 seconds.
  • Week 3: duality, GP, SDP. Write the Lagrangian and dual for a small LP and QP without thinking.

Weeks 4–5 — algorithms and mock exams

  • Week 4: gradient vs Newton vs interior point. Recognize which method a problem calls for; run one or two iterations by hand.
  • Week 5: stop learning new material. Full mocks under exam conditions, gap-closing, exam tactics. Light review the day before, sleep.

Exam-day tactics

The marking is +1 / −⅓ / 0 over roughly 11–12 multiple-choice questions. A blind guess on 4 options breaks even, so never guess blind — but if you can eliminate even one option, answering has positive expected value.

  • Read all questions first. Mark each E (easy), M (medium), or H (hard). Do all the E's, then the M's, then the H's only where you can eliminate an option.
  • Leaving 3–4 blanks is fine. Eight correct with two wrong already clears 18.
  • About 4–5 minutes per question. If you have spent 7 on one, flag it and move on.
  • Do not change answers on instinct at the end — only change if you find a concrete mistake.
